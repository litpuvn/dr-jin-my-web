Fang Jin, Ph.D. Assistant Professor

Fang Jin joined Texas Tech University in the Spring of 2017. She received her Ph.D. degree from The Department of Computer Science at Virginia Tech in 2016. She was supervised by Dr. Naren Ramakrishnan in the Discovery Analytics Center. She has a broad interest in Data Mining and Machine Learning. Her research has been focused on information propagation modeling, graph mining, anomaly detection, and spatiotemporal data analysis.

Long Nguyen Ph.D. Student

Long received his Bachelor’s degree in Information Technology at the President University (Indonesia) in 2005 and then his Master’s degree in Computer Science at Politecnico di Milano, Italy in 2009. He had worked over 10 years in software industry in different countries (such as Singapore, Vietnam, and Italy) before starting to pursue PhD degree in Jan 2017. He has a great interest in data analytics especially regarding to text and social network data.

Zhou Yang Ph.D. Student

Zhou Yang received his bachelor’s degree in Management (Management Science) at Wuhan University (China) in 2013, and he graduated from University of Florida with a master’s degree in Industrial Engineering in 2015. Before pursuing his Ph.D degree at Texas Tech University in August 2017, he was a big data analyst in China Unicom (China). He has a great interest in machine learning and its application to industries.

Sisheng Liang Ph.D. Student

Sisheng Liang received his Bachelor’s degree in Automation at Northeastern University (China) in 2008 and his first Master's degree in Control Theory and Control Engineering at Tongji University (China) in 2011. He was a design engineer at SAIC Motor Corporation Limited. He received his second Master's degree in Electrical Engineering at Texas Tech University in 2018. He started to pursue his PhD degree in Computer Science at Texas Tech University in Fall 2018. He has great interests in data analytics and text mining in social media context.
